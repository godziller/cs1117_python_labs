Overview

Where is the Kerry Way?

The Kerry way is in Kerry.

What is the Kerry Way?

The Kerry Way is a 200+ km hike around the Iveragh Peninsula.

Why should you hike the Kerry Way?

You will summit peaks, paddle in sparkling bays, and forage in forests, all
the time marvelling at spectacular Irish countryside. The only downside is
that you start and end in Killarney.

When is the best time to hike the Kerry Way?

Daytime is the best time to hike the Kerry Way.

In what direction should you hike the Kerry Way?

It all depends on your orientation. If you’re a clockwise dude, then it’s
better for you to drive the Ring of Kerry; if you swing the other way, then
hiking the Kerry Way is for you.

Safety

What to watch out for!

Walking has its dangers:

  • Bears! Bears are mammals with large bodies, shaggy hair, short
    tails, sharp claws, and bad teeth. Most are omnivores, which means
    they enjoy fresh hiker with a side order of vegetable matter.
    However, there are no bears in Ireland.
  • Snakes! Snakes are limbless reptiles, found on all continents except
    for Antarctica. A snake can swallow prey that is larger than its head,
    which is worrying for small hikers. However, there are no snakes in Ireland.
  • Wolves! Wolves are like big, devil-eyed dogs. They hunt hikers in packs,
    encircling any laggards in your party and tearing them limb from limb.
    However, there are no wolves in Ireland.

No bears, no snakes, no wolves. I’m not sure why I included this section.

Do and Donts

  • Do's
    • Pack out what you pack in.
    • You must tell someone of your plans.
  • Dont's
    • Don’t wear denim or flip flops or clashing colours.
    • Don’t disturb the wildlife, including the locals.

Culture

Star Wars fans will know that The Last Jedi features shots of the Skellig Islands,
which can be seen during your hike.

Less well known is that the hotel in The Lobster is the Parknasilla Resort and Spa,
just outside of Sneem village.

Finally, Lough Leanne is the place where Oisín fell in love with Niamh.
After 300 ageless loved-up years together in Tír na nÓg, Oisín returned in
search of his family. But, on touching Irish soil, he aged in an instant and
died soon after, buried by St. Patrick. So let that be a lesson to you.
