Radiohead

Radiohead is a band that ticks every box. Know for their experimental compositions, Thom Yorkes harrowing and catatonic
vocals, Guitar peices that are complex yet simple, drums that often hold a complex pattern, bass that often supplies the very
power to the bands persona. More here

  1. Vocals

    I find the vocals in their music to always compliment the other instruments. Thom's voice is one of a very upset or
    mundane character, but remains a versatile one. He can be seen going from signature, weightless, depressing melodies
    to energetic, wild, desperate "shout" singing. Most of the bands vocal recordings hold a very present and close tone.
    That meaning that the vocals seem close to the users ears with little use of reverb although some tracks do contain it,
    if not heavy reverb, as i said the band is extremely experimental, each album differing from their ther works

    In an interview Thom told the reporter that, most of the time it isnt his intention to make their songs sounds so sad
    its just how his voice sounds, its his vessel for art. Art, indeed it is art. Another noticable thing about Radioheads vocals is
    the often surreal, poetic style of writing lyrics. Each song tells of odd, almost bizarre combinations of words, ideas
    and scenes. The easiest way i can describe them is like: an aroma that you smell in the strangest place which reminds you
    of something oddly nostalgic or emotional.

  2. Guitar and Bass

    There are two guitarists in Radiohead but i will be talking about the guitar singularly.
    the guitar in Radiohead often bear a clean, close and sensual. Soft picking, to far ambient
    drones and moans. the guitar has also been seen experimenting with distortion, most famously on
    "Creep" which I think just about everyone knows. Ironically thats actually pretty uncommon for
    the lads, distortion wasnt really consistant on any record besides maybe the bends in which had a
    lot noisier, rocky mood about it

    Bearing similarity to bands like "The Beatles" instruments (often guitar) were hard paned to one ear.
    for me the most iconic Radiohead tone of guitar would either be

    1. A close picked and panned electric guitar with little effects running through it
    2. A far and ambient sounds with heavy reverb, chorus, perhaps delay and a stereo or moving sound
    3. A close acoustic guitar which is often centered, being the centre of that song

    On the other foot I find the bass playing to be hard to classify. In terms of tone its almost always a close
    and wife clean tone which is very organic sounding, grounding the bands songs in a sense of reality where in
    some songs without the bass would otherwise be loose and unorganised. The bass is integral to their songs.
    Most parts are often quite simple to quite complex, ranging from some root notes and passing notes to odd patterns
    and un syncopated rhythm

  3. Drums

    There is a song that Radio head called Reckoner, that i often find myself trying to tap out
    the rhythm and cant. The drums in their music is a high blend of highly complex syncopation and simple ground rhythm.
    I find that due to the drums and beat most Radiohead songs are relatively danceable or bopable.
